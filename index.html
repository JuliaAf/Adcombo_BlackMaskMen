the secret of the samurai

The Japanese samurai were usually involved in long military campaigns without being able to wash with soap and water. However, The Bushido code of honour obliged the warriors to keep their souls and bodies clean. That's when the samurai learned how to use bamboo charcoal instead of soap. Fires for cooking were usually made with bamboo, so there was always plenty of charcoal at hand.

Charcoal absorbed sebum and killed bacteria, leaving the skin dry and clean.

When the wars were over, the samurai continued to use charcoal, considering it to be the best hygiene product.
